– Non verbal communication –

Talking has not been the only way of communication for a long time of history, painting, pointing and even morse code are all different ways of communicating that humans have invented, Humans have not only been communicating verbally but rather they have been communicating in countless different ways all that serve a purpose:

  1. Art:
    • Art has been used through history in order to communicate and pass down information to younger generations,
    • Art has allowed humans to express themselves in a colorful abstract way that is not very easy to do with verbal communication,
    • Art has been around humans as early as 40,000 years ago helping us express ourselves and adapt to the world around us.
  2. Secret code:
    • Secrets have always been a part of human nature for purposes to either protect ourselves or the people around us, that is why many different types of secret codes have been made throughout history
    • Codes like Morse code started in early 1800 by Samuel Morse and Alfred vail these codes were used with a series of quick and long beeps to represent the english alphabet
    • Codes have been around us for a long time and they could arguably be just as important as saying the truth, because for wars and secret operations they have been used throughout history
  3. Signaling:
    • Signaling has been used by humanity for ages, we have since the beginning used our hands to point at stuff and to communicate.
    • Many deaf people communicate via signals and understand the world around them via these signals, furthermore signaling is core in the nature of communication as it is almost impossible to communicate without doing any type of hand movement.
    • A signal is basically anything that we do with our bodies in order to communicate more efficiently, for example facial expression is one kind of signal and a very important one.
